Much of the punk rock, ska and two-tone music created by British artists at the time was influenced by Margret Thatchers impact on society, the Falkan war that was occurring during the time, the AIDS/HIV crisis, and the culture of Britain being one of discrimination and poverty. Many songs commented on the struggle that had occurred for the last decade, as Britain had been falling into an economic downfall. A popular punk band, Sex Pistols, created many songs commenting on the Monarchy of Britain, and the general anarchy that was spawning in towns due to brewing anger against discrimination and the government. The Sex Pistols created their music before Margaret Thatcher came into power, however, their songs were heavily influenced by the history of Britain and the hatred that had been forming in popular culture. Once Margret Thatcher had come into power, many songs commented on her, many referencing her by name. The punk movement revolved around an anti-capitalist mindset, which allowed many to disengage from the high interest rates and unemployment that were occurring. Now, the punk movement is still an incredibly popular movement, with many still supporting the ideas of the subculture.

The punk movement assisted in changing the views and social norms of British society with the subculture's view of acceptance and support for everyone. It also assisted in promoting protests as a means of calling attention to an issue in society or parliament, which are still used today as a means of standing against an issue.
